    <h2>About</h2>
    <p>
      I am a Research Assistant Professor in the
      <a href="https://mae.hkust.edu.hk/en/people/faculty/detail/lin-pengzhu">Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering</a>
      at <a href="https://hkust.edu.hk/">The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology</a>.
      I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from HKUST in 2024, and my M.Eng. and B.Eng.
      degrees from <a href="https://www.hust.edu.cn/">Huazhong University of Science and Technology</a>.
    </p>
    <p>
      My research develops multiscale and AI-assisted models for energy storage and conversion systems,
      with a focus on heat and mass transport in fuel cells, batteries, and water electrolyzers.
      I am particularly interested in connecting pore-scale structure, transport physics, and data-driven
      optimization to guide high-performance electrochemical devices.
    </p>
    <p>
      My work has appeared in journals including <em>Nature Communications</em>, <em>Science Bulletin</em>,
      <em>ACS Energy Letters</em>, <em>Applied Energy</em>, and <em>International Journal of Heat and Mass Transfer</em>.
    </p>
  </div>
